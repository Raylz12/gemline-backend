01 · WHERE WE STAND

The product is built. The business wrapper isn’t.

Honest one-paragraph status, then the numbers.

GEMLINE is a feature-complete marketplace running in Stripe test mode. The entire money loop — checkout, escrow, shipping confirmation, 48-hour inspection, settlement, payouts, refunds, disputes — has been proven end-to-end twice with automated test runs. The catalog, price engine, and discovery features are live in production today and publicly usable. What’s missing is not software: it’s the legal entity, live payment rails, shipping labels, tax posture, and a handful of policy decisions. That’s what this document maps out.

The one-sentence business model

Collectors use the free price guide and collection tools (acquisition); they buy and sell cards through escrow-protected checkout where we take 10% of each sale (regular sellers) or $10/month + 5% (shop accounts); premium analytics can later flip to a paid Pro tier with a one-line config change.

What “verifiable business” means here

A real legal entity

Gemline LLC (Florida), EIN, operating agreement, registered agent, business bank account. Customers, Stripe, and the IRS all see a real company — not a hobby.

Live, verified payment rails

Stripe account fully activated (identity + bank verified), Stripe Connect for seller payouts, live webhooks, and a real-money end-to-end transaction before public launch.

Compliant operations

Sales tax posture decided, 1099-K reporting handled via Stripe, terms & privacy naming the real entity, age policy, dispute policy, and insurance appropriate to stage.

Provable trust for users

Escrow held by Stripe (never by us personally), verified shipping with tracking, published fees, real support channel, and refund/dispute processes that actually run.

Bottom line: nothing on the critical path requires new engineering. The remaining work is roughly 2–3 weeks of paperwork, account setup, and policy decisions, most of which only Rhett can legally do — with scripts and integrations on my side already written or scoped.

04 · LEGAL & COMPLIANCE PATH

Doing it by the book — without overbuilding.

What each compliance area actually requires at our stage, in plain English.

Entity & taxes

  • Florida LLC (~$125 filing + ~$100/yr registered agent). Terms already specify FL law.
  • EIN free from IRS online. Opens bank + Stripe.
  • Single-member LLC = pass-through taxes; S-corp election later when profit justifies it.
  • Bookkeeping from day one (Wave is free; QuickBooks ~$35/mo). Keep business money separate.
  • Annual report to FL each year (~$139) — calendar it.

Payments & money handling

  • Escrow is held by Stripe, not by us (manual-capture intents). This keeps us out of money-transmitter licensing territory — keep it that way.
  • Stripe Connect Express gives every seller their own verified payout identity; Stripe handles KYC on sellers.
  • 1099-K reporting for sellers is handled by Stripe under Connect — near-zero burden on us.
  • Chargebacks: our escrow + tracking evidence flow already produces what Stripe disputes need.

Sales tax (the one to not ignore)

  • As a marketplace facilitator, most states make the marketplace collect & remit once economic nexus is hit (commonly $100K sales or 200 transactions per state per year).
  • At launch volume we’re under every threshold — but the clean play is Stripe Tax (0.5% of taxed volume) turned on early: it calculates, collects & tells us where to register.
  • Register in Florida first (home state), others only as thresholds approach.

Users, minors & privacy

  • We market to young collectors — so the rule matters: accounts require 13+ (COPPA). Under-13s browse public pages; buying/selling needs a parent’s account.
  • Add explicit age language to Terms during the lawyer pass; consider an age checkbox at signup (5-line change).
  • Privacy policy already live; confirm it names the entity, lists Stripe/Resend/Cloudflare as processors, and offers a deletion contact.

Brand protection

  • USPTO trademark on “GEMLINE” for online marketplace services (~$250–350/class, DIY-able). Not launch-blocking; cheap insurance once traction shows.
  • We already avoided the big IP landmine by retiring virtual packs using Topps/Panini branding — real physical cards only.

Platform policies

  • Published fees (10% / shop tier), 3-day ship rule, 48h inspection — all live.
  • Prohibited items list (counterfeits, trimmed cards) — add during lawyer pass.
  • Dispute policy decision (refund vs release default) → then it’s just enforcement.

Reality check: a Florida LLC + EIN + bank + activated Stripe + updated terms puts us on par, compliance-wise, with how virtually every marketplace startup launches. Stripe Tax + insurance + trademark are the “growing up” layer we add as GMV appears.

Revenue & breakeven

Fixed burn lands around $100–170/mo post-launch. At a 10% take rate that’s ~$1,200–1,700/mo in GMV to break even — roughly 15–20 average card sales, or 10–17 shop subscriptions before counting their sales at all. Every layer above that (Pro tier, more shops, auction volume) is margin.

Top risks & how we handle them

Cold-start liquidity

A marketplace with no listings sells nothing. Counter: shop-account outreach (handouts), seed our own inventory, free price-guide funnel already pulling collectors in.

Fraud & fakes

Counterfeits and INR scams are the category’s plague. Counter: escrow + tracking + inspection window + scan-to-verify + dispute freeze — all built; add prohibited-items policy in lawyer pass.

Data dependency

Prices ride on one provider. Counter: our own price history accumulates daily (bulk exports archived); eBay API keys already provisioned as secondary signal.

Key-person ops

Automation runs itself, but decisions bottleneck on Rhett. Counter: this document — plus admin tooling & kill switches that make interventions one-click.

Open decisions sitting on Rhett’s desk

  • Dispute default: refund the buyer or release to seller when evidence is thin?
  • Sales tax: flip on Stripe Tax early (clean, 0.5%) or defer until volume?
  • Images: bulk-source the 1.31M missing thumbs or launch with placeholders (current)?
  • Trademark: file GEMLINE now (~$350) or after traction?

Recommended next action: finish the LLC papers. Literally everything else on the critical path is waiting behind that one envelope. The day it clears: EIN same day, bank same week, Stripe live within days — and the first real card sells the week after.
